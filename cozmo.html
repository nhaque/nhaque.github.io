Coding with Cozmo
A 2-week course for middleschoolers on programming basics with Cozmo


CONTEXT


Computer Science Education is still highly inaccessible at schools. Majority of the students are first exposed to Computer Science in the early high school years with an introduction to basic programming which requires a huge learning curve and its unfamiliarity could result in students losing motivation and interest. This decrease is even more sever for female students who plan to pursue a STEM career.

Role

Instructional designer

Tools and Methods

Adobe CC, Backwards Design, Cognitive Walkthroughs


PROCESS


Personas


I employed Backwards Design; starting with assessment design and ending with evaluation. The content was then iterated after research through cognitive walkthroughs and speed dating. From my research, it was concluded that students often don't have a solid foundation of the basic programming concepts and the platforms utilized often has a high learning curve, resulting in lack of interest. Hence, I used the Cozmo robot as a platform to sustain the learner's engagement as well as master the basic concepts through instruction.




FINAL DELIVERABLE


I created a 2-week long course with challenge cards and instruction that covers the basic programming concepts like conditionals and repeat loops.
