Chocolate Therapy
Emotion based online game


OVERVIEW


Chocolate therapy is a therapeutic experimental game about emotions. Through the game, the player experiences life events of the protagonists who has lost all his memories by completing each level and the end goal is to create a chocolate for his loved one and uncover the true nature of their relationship.

Team

Nabeeha Fatima, Henry Chang, April Xu

Role

UX Designer and Researcher

Tools

Vue.js, Game Design, Iterative Prototyping






PROCESS


Ideation

I brainstormed 50 different ideas which was then refined and narrowed down to explore the theme of emotions and colors (we went with the former).




Prototypes

The initial prototypes were created using Figma, which later went through iterations. For the first prototype, we wanted to focus on gameplay mechanics and hence created a level based on the joy emotion




The motivation behind the prototype was to explore if the ‘joy’ game level concept made sense to them and explore ways to make it more fun. For the later iterations, we used different versions of the game (listed below) where we focused on refining level 1 (joy) which also acts as a tutorial of sorts and then we proceeded with the later level ideas.


Testing and Feedback


We, then, conducted 17 playtests which included classmates and family members over a span of 2 weeks and made changes utilizing speedboat analysis.




REFLECTION


Learning by Doing
It is crucial to brainstorm smartly, otherwise, you end up spending a lot of time ideating. Providing constraints for ideas helps you learn more by doing them via testing in smaller batches which in turn narrows down your selection for the most desirable idea.
