Metamorphosis
Helping snowboarders express themselves through their boards


Problem


During the off-season and considering the current COVID lockdown situation, with resorts shutdown,snowboarders have their equipment unused. Gilson aims to deepen the relationship with their existing snowboard community during this period and also investigate ideas oh how to branch out to those who are also snowboarders with boards from other brands.

How might we support engagement between Gilson and the snowboarder community after a board has been purchased?

Team

Nabeeha Fatima, Jocelyn Guo, Alex Heyison, Anton Vekhov

Role

UX Designer and Researcher

Client

Gilson Snow

Methods

Semi-structured interviews, Speed Dating, User Journey Mapping, Service Blueprinting


Solution


The Metamorphosis Service un-bundles artwork from the physical board Riders easily apply new topskins and decals inspired by professional and amateur artists and the Brands Gilson loves.





Process





1. Exploratory Research

Having never snowboarded before, as a first step,I went to the local ski resort, Seven Springs, as a field trip and experienced it to capture what the snowboarders feel. I also talked with other resort visitors to gather information as to how did they get started and what entailed their sbowboard purchasing journey. The team, as a whole, then reached out to the Gilson Facebook community and interviewed current customers as well as team riders and ambassadors.



Key Insights

01.

Snowboarders feel a personal connection to the graphics on their snowboards.

02.

Snowboarders tend to rally together to discuss their boards, styles and favorite spots both online and offline

03.

Snowboarders purchase a new snowboard if they want to try a different style or if they have damaged their existing one.
