Metamorphosis
Helping snowboarders express themselves through their boards


PROBLEM


During the off-season and considering the current COVID lockdown situation, with resorts shutdown,snowboarders have their equipment unused. Gilson aims to deepen the relationship with their existing snowboard community during this period and also investigate ideas oh how to branch out to those who are also snowboarders with boards from other brands.

How might we support engagement between Gilson and the snowboarder community after a board has been purchased?

Team

Nabeeha Fatima, Jocelyn Guo, Alex Heyison, Anton Vekhov

Role

UX Designer and Researcher

Client

Gilson Snow

Methods

Semi-structured interviews, Speed Dating, User Journey Mapping, Service Blueprinting


SOLUTION


The Metamorphosis Service un-bundles artwork from the physical board Riders easily apply new topskins and decals inspired by professional and amateur artists and the Brands Gilson loves.







PROCESS





1. Exploratory Research

Having never snowboarded before, as a first step,I went to the local ski resort, Seven Springs, as a field trip and experienced it to capture what the snowboarders feel. I also talked with other resort visitors to gather information as to how did they get started and what entailed their sbowboard purchasing journey. The team, as a whole, then reached out to the Gilson Facebook community and interviewed current customers as well as team riders and ambassadors.



Key Insights

01.

Snowboarders feel a personal connection to the graphics on their snowboards.

02.

Snowboarders tend to rally together to discuss their boards, styles and favorite spots both online and offline

03.

Snowboarders purchase a new snowboard if they want to try a different style or if they have damaged their existing one.

2. Define



despite experiencing success in the online community, Gilson still had a huge potential in further leveraging those platforms that would impact various touch points surrounding the Gilson snowboard.




3. Ideation

I conducted a brainstorming session where we came up with 20 different ideas based on the insights generated, audience type, and pain points identified in the service blueprint. Since the ideas were vastly different from each other, there were two rounds of voting and discussion to narrow down the selection.



The team and I decided to then focus on the top 6 ideas and whiteboard them as potential pitches.


  • Ideas 1 & 2
  • Ideas 3 & 4
  • Ideas 5 & 6


Feedback from Client

Unfortunately, the client rejected most of our ideas due to differences in priorities and was looking for small changes that would create a large impact and wanted to focus more on leveraging engagement of existing audience.

“We definitely want to go deeper with the groups we’re already winning with. But if we only do that we’re going to be a small company. We have to expand to new groups. We’re winning with 16 year olds and only have 4% penetration, so we have to go deeper there. We also look to pry people away who are already snowboarders and bring them to Gilson.”

Based on the feedback received from the client, we went back to our research data and the blueprint map to ideate based on the following areas of opportunity:
(a) Find a way to foster engagement between Gilson and their riders AFTER a board purchase
(b) Leverage the artistic activity and personalized appeal of the Gilson Custom program independently of the board



Identifying Unbundled Value

Our team undertook a simple concept mapping exercise to help identify the potential of unbundling artwork since Gilson had mentioned the success of their custom line as a growth opportunity and uniquely suited to their production paradigm.



Key Insights

01.

Gilson Artists will have a more direct line to riders

02.

Unbundled artwork can be community designed and community voted

03.

We have to be careful with the term “sticker.” It cheapens the idea.

04.

Boards have to come in one piece. Artwork doesn’t. Art can grow!

05.

Brands sponsor artwork, not fiberglass.  More frequent artwork = more frequent  $ponsorship

06.

Lots of segments. Riders who are sick of their old design + riders who want to keep their board update


Final Solution Idea

After consolidating the new insights and client approval, we refined our solution as follows:

Metamorphosis Service un-bundles artwork from the physical board
Riders easily apply new topskins and decals inspired by professional and amateur artists and the Brands Gilson loves.




4. Prototyping and Testing

To test out the solution, I created wireframes along mid-fi to hi-fi prototypes. For iteration and feedback, I conducted usability studies with SUS and think-aloud with 4 participants.

REFLECTION


Focus on the process not the idea

A good designer always iterates their ideas by incorporating several processes. Ideas that seem like they would work in the initial stage may not always work out during the later stages in the project.

Listen, Listen and Listen more

It’s absolutely essential to truly listen in a meaningful and a thoughful manner for a designer. First and foremost, listening to the team is crucial since there are members of diverse backgrounds and varying experiences. You need to “listen” to your data – this is where the insights play a huge role since they not only set the pace for future research but also helps you understand the users. Lastly, “listen” to your mentors/colleagues – seek them out for feedback and guidance; it never hurts to get an expert review.
